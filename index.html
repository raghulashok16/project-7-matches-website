Candle Wax

It is a material that is used as fuel for candles. It is made from a variety of sources, including petroleum, beeswax, and soy, and it comes in different forms, such as pellets, blocks, and liquid. When heated, candle wax melts and vaporizes, releasing heat and light. The type of wax used and the way it is made affects the properties of the candle, such as its burn time, scent, and appearance.

Safety matches

It is a type of match that is designed to prevent accidental ignition. They are made with a special striking surface, usually on the box, that is separate from the matchstick itself. This means that the matchstick can only be lit by striking it against the designated striking surface, making it safer to use than traditional matches. Safety matches are commonly used for lighting candles, stoves, and fireplaces. They are widely available and come in a variety of sizes.
